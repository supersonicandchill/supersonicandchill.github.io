Not Today — Privacy Policy

Last updated July 2026

The short version: Not Today does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data, from you or about you. There are no accounts, no analytics, no ad networks, and no third-party trackers of any kind.

What the app stores

Your streaks, habit names, and settings are saved only on your own device, using Apple's standard local storage APIs. This data never leaves your device, is never sent to any server, and is not accessible to the developer or anyone else. If you delete the app, this data is erased and cannot be recovered unless you've used the app's built-in "Back up my streaks" export feature to save a copy yourself.

Notifications

If you turn on the optional evening or morning reminders, they are scheduled entirely on your device using Apple's local notification system. No notification content or scheduling data is sent to any external service.

External links

If you enable the optional faith mode, tapping a Bible chapter link opens Bible Gateway in your device's browser. That visit is subject to Bible Gateway's own privacy policy, not this one, and only happens if you choose to tap a link.

Backups you create

The app's export feature lets you copy your own data out using Apple's share sheet — for example, to send yourself a backup file. Where that file goes after that (Messages, Notes, email, etc.) is controlled entirely by you and the app you choose to send it to.

Children's privacy

Not Today does not knowingly collect information from anyone, of any age, because it does not collect information at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the update will be posted here with a new "last updated" date.
